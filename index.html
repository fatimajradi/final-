NLP

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence (AI) that focuses on the interaction between computers and human language. The primary goal of NLP is to enable computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a way that is both meaningful and contextually relevant. NLP involves a wide range of techniques and components to achieve this, and its applications span from chatbots and language translation to sentiment analysis and information extraction

Tokenization

Tokenization is a fundamental step in NLP, breaking down text into individual units (words or tokens). It provides the foundation for subsequent analyses and is essential for understanding the structure of language.

Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging:

Assigning grammatical categories to each token helps in understanding the syntactic structure of sentences. POS tagging is a key building block for more complex NLP tasks and applications.

Named Entity Recognition (NER):

Identifying and classifying entities (such as names of people, organizations, locations) is crucial for extracting meaningful information from text. NER is particularly important in applications involving information extraction and knowledge base construction.
